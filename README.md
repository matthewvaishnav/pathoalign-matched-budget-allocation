# PathoAlign matched-budget allocation study

This repository is a focused study package for the PathoAlign matched-budget allocation experiment.

The question is narrow: under matched total pair-presentation budget, does spending budget on more unique biological pairs improve the biological representation more than spending the same budget on repeated anchors?

## Supported claim

Under matched total pair-presentation budgets of 6,400 and 12,800 pair presentations, increasing biological pair diversity improves PathoAlign biological consistency and factor separation relative to low-diversity/high-repetition anchor allocation, with a plateau from 100 to 200 pairs. Doubling the total budget from 6,400 to 12,800 also improves the biological score.

This is a representation-identifiability and resource-allocation study. It is not clinical validation, diagnostic software, or evidence of patient-care utility.
